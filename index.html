1. Fort Bonifacio Global City


Wear your best outfits, and remember to snap a pic
in the most Instagrammable place in Taguig, Bonifacio Global City!
This former military training ground turned business district
is now home to the tallest skyscrapers in the country.

3. St. Anne Parish Church


Taguig City is known for its new skyscrapers and modern establishments, but the St. Anne
Parish Church grants us a glimpse of the city back in the day. Known locally as Santa Ana
Church or simply Taguig Church, this Baroque-style church dates back to 1571, with the
current building established in 1883.

To know more about Taguig's history during the Spanish Colonial Era and the story of Santa
Ana Church, you can visit the museum within the church itself. You'll also find religious
relics stored in the Santa Ana Museum.
